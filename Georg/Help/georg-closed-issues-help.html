Georg - Closed Issues List

This page displays all resolved and closed complaints and claims in your system. It serves as a historical record of past issues and their resolutions, providing valuable data for analysis and process improvement.

Important: Closed issues are not editable unless reopened. To make changes to a closed issue, you must first return it to "Processing" status.

Working with Closed Issues

From this list view, you can:

View Resolution Details

Click on any closed issue to see the full complaint record, including all resolution actions taken. This provides valuable insight into successful resolution approaches.

Filter and Search

Use the filters to find specific issues by date, customer, category, or any other available field. This makes it easy to locate past issues for reference or to identify patterns.

Export to Excel

Export the data for further analysis and reporting in Microsoft Excel. This is particularly useful for creating detailed reports on resolution times, common issues, and performance metrics.

Reopen if Needed

If necessary, you can reopen a closed issue by changing its status back to "Processing." This is useful when follow-up action is required for a previously closed complaint.

Analysis and Reporting

The closed issues list is invaluable for performance analysis and process improvement. Here are some ways to leverage this data:

Resolution Time Analysis

Compare registration and closure dates to measure response times. This helps identify:

  • Average time to resolution
  • Issues that took longer than expected
  • Improvement or decline in resolution speed over time

Pattern Identification

Look for common factors among complaints:

  • Recurring reasons or categories
  • Business units handling the most issues
  • Origins of most frequent complaints

Performance Metrics

Track key performance indicators:

  • Resolution rates by team or individual
  • Number of reopened issues
  • Issues resolved within target timeframes

Customer Impact Assessment

Evaluate customer-related patterns:

  • Customers with highest complaint volumes
  • Issue types by customer segment
  • Correlation between complaints and customer retention

Pro Tip: Export your closed issues to Excel on a monthly or quarterly basis for trend analysis. This can help identify systemic issues that might need addressing at an organizational level.

Learning from Past Issues

Closed issues provide valuable learning opportunities for your team:

  1. Review successful resolution approaches for similar future issues
  2. Identify root causes that might need systemic fixes
  3. Recognize staff members who consistently resolve issues effectively
  4. Document best practices based on successful resolutions
  5. Identify training needs based on recurring issues

Note: Consider implementing a regular review process for closed issues to ensure continuous improvement in your complaint handling procedures. A monthly review meeting can be an effective way to share learnings across the team.
